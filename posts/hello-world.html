Hello, world — why I'm starting this site

Every now and then I have things worth writing down. This is where I'll do that.

I've been meaning to start a personal site for a while. Not because I have some grand audience in mind or anything I desperately need to say — but because writing forces clarity. You don't really know what you think about something until you've tried to explain it.

The plan is loose. I'll write about things I'm working on, books or articles I found interesting, career stuff, random observations. Probably a mix that won't fit neatly into a single category. That's fine.

"Writing is thinking. To write well is to think clearly. That's why it's so hard." — David McCullough

What this site is

A place to think out loud. A record of what I'm paying attention to at different points in my life. Something I'll look back on in five years and cringe at, hopefully in a good way.

I'm not trying to build a brand or grow an audience. If you stumble across this and something resonates, great. If not, that's fine too.

What's coming

I'll write as things come up. Some posts will be long, some short. Some polished, some more like rough notes. I'll add more pages to the site over time — projects I'm working on, things I'm interested in, that kind of thing.

For now: hello, world.
